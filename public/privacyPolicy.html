Definitions:

  • Personal Data: Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.

  • Processing: Any operation or set of operations which is performed on Personal Data or on sets of Personal Data.

2. Information We Collect and Process

To provide and enhance our Services, we collect several types of information from and about our users. The collection is limited to what is necessary for the legitimate purposes outlined in this policy.

2.1. Data Provided Directly by You

  • User Content: This refers to the code, text, queries, or other data snippets that you voluntarily input into our tools for processing ("User Content"). Our core privacy principle is that we do not permanently store or log the substantive content of your User Content. Processing is performed in-memory or via transient, stateless API calls. Once the Service has performed the requested manipulation and delivered the output, the input User Content is programmatically discarded. We do not use your User Content for any purpose other than fulfilling your direct request. You are strongly cautioned against entering any sensitive data, trade secrets, credentials, or protected health information into our tools.

2.2. Data Collected Automatically (Usage Data)

6. Cookie Policy and User Choices

6.1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by websites that you visit. They are widely used to make websites work, or work more efficiently, as well as to provide information to the owners of the site.

6.2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for the following purposes:

  • Strictly Necessary Cookies: These cookies are essential for you to browse the website and use its features. Without these, services like remembering your session cannot be provided.
  • Functionality Cookies: These are used to recognize you when you return to our website and to remember your preferences. For example, we use a cookie (`theme`) to store your preference for dark or light mode, so you do not have to set it on every visit.
  • Performance/Analytics Cookies: These cookies collect information about how you use our website, like which pages you visited and which links you clicked on. None of this information can be used to identify you. It is all aggregated and, therefore, anonymized. Their sole purpose is to improve website functions.

6.3. Managing Cookies

Most web browsers allow some control of most cookies through the browser settings. You can set your browser to refuse all cookies or to indicate when a cookie is being sent. However, if you do not accept cookies, you may not be able to use some portions of our Service (for example, theme persistence).
